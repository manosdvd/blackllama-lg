## Adult Showers
Located just behind the Nature Lodge, the Adult Showers are separate facilities strictly reserved for adult leaders. These ADA-compliant structures feature flush toilets and are divided into separate men's and women's sides. Youth are not permitted in these facilities without specific medical/ADA authorization.

## Al's Well
Named for Al Christensen who built the means to utilize the three springs near camp (known as Boy Scout Spring, Girl Scout Spring, and Lower Spring), Al's Well is an iconic landmark and drinking fountain on the trail to the youth shower house.

## Apache Campsite
Apache is a mid-sized campsite that can accommodate up to 11 campers.

## Archery Range
Part of the Range and Target Activities (RATA) area, the Archery Range allows Scouts to hone their marksmanship and earn the Archery merit badge. Access requires strict obedience to Range Director commands and the use of all required protective equipment.

## Black Ogre
The Black Ogre is the historical and colorful name given to the Ranger's tool shed and maintenance shop. 

## Camp Office
Home of the camp administration, the Camp Office is a central hub for communication and management records, located near the main parking lot. The new patio area now allows for meetings and leisure. All visitors must immediately sign in here upon arrival.

## Campfire Bowl
The Campfire Bowl is the gathering place for the camp's opening and closing programs. It hosts the Friday night Camper Campfire, where troops perform pre-approved skits and songs, as well as the staff campfires.

## Chapel
The Clayton Herbert Memorial Chapel is an outdoor worship space open to all units and individuals for observing the twelfth point of the Scout Law. Camp staff lead a short, non-denominational interfaith service here on Sunday evenings.

## Cochise Campsite
Cochise is a smaller, more intimate campsite capable of holding up to 8 campers.

## Dining Hall
With a commercial-quality kitchen and space for the entire camp, the dining hall is the central hub for meals and daily announcements. It also serves as the primary shelter during severe storms and lightning. The kitchen area is strictly restricted; campers and leaders may not enter without explicit permission from the Cook or Camp Director.

## Drinking Fountain (Peek Memorial)
Located near the center of camp, this drinking fountain was originally built in the late 1940s by Scouts and Scouters from Hayden, AZ, in memory of Scoutmaster Leonard L. Peek. It was lovingly rebuilt in 2012 by former Camp Lawton staff members.

## Eagle Quest
Eagle Quest is the upper equivalent to Model Camp, committed to helping Scouts earn their Eagle rank. Located at the Eagle's Nest, this area focuses on essential required badges like Citizenship in the Nation, Citizenship in the World, Citizenship in the Community, and Communications.

## Flammables Storage
A secure facility designated for the safe storage of all flammable materials and chemicals used in camp operations and maintenance.

## Front Gate
The main entrance to Camp Lawton is named for James M. Lawton, a former Catalina Council President for whom the camp is named. 

## Gila Campsite
Gila is the largest individual campsite on the property, capable of accommodating up to 17 campers.

## Handicraft
Handicraft is the creative center of camp, operating on a largely independent study schedule. Scouts can work on artistic skills and earn badges like Art, Basketry, Leatherwork, and Wood Carving.

## Health Lodge
The Health Lodge is the domain of the Camp Health Officer (Medic) and the central location for treating injuries and illnesses. All prescription and over-the-counter medications for both youth and adults must be stored securely here and are distributed by the medical staff.

## Hohokam Campsite
Hohokam is a smaller campsite located on the southern edge of the property, with a capacity of 7 campers.

## Hopi Campsite
Hopi is the smallest designated campsite on the property, accommodating up to 6 campers.

## Latrines/KYBOs
Each campsite (or campsite group) has a KYBO (Keep Your Bowels Open). KYBOs are what we call latrines/outhouses/restrooms. They are traditional non-flushing pit toilets, but private individual units with lockable doors. Each structure also has a sink, water faucet, hose, and a drinking fountain. **It is the unit's responsibility to keep them clean**.

## Model Camp
Model Camp is the camp's premier Trail to First Class program. Designed as the ideal setup for newer Scouts, it offers self-paced instruction to help first and second-year campers complete early rank requirements.

## Nature
Also known as the Ecology area, the Nature Lodge is dedicated to the study of the natural world. Instructors here teach Merit Badges such as Environmental Science, Astronomy, Forestry, Mammal Study, and Plant Science.

## Navajo Campsite
The northeastern campsite is well suited for special needs units with vehicle access and no difficult trails to reach. It has a capacity to accommodate up to 12 campers.

## Oglala Campsite
Surrounded by old-growth forest, ferns, and a creek bed, the Oglala campsite is arguably the most beautiful of the campsites in camp. It has a capacity of 10 campers.

## Parade Grounds
Located at the camp's flagpoles, the Parade Grounds serve as the mandatory assembly area for morning and evening flag ceremonies. Crucially, it is also the primary gathering point for the entire camp in the event of a continuous bell alarm or emergency drill.

## Pima Campsite
Sister site to Oglala, Pima is a bit further back but equally nice. It accommodates up to 8 campers.

## Quartermasters
The Quartermasters area serves as the central supply and logistics hub for camp operations, storing gear, tools, and program materials.

## Ranger's Cabin
Built in 1957 by a massive effort of volunteers, contractors, Order of the Arrow members, and Scouts, this cabin serves as the on-site residence for the Camp Ranger.

## Rendezvous Area
The Rendezvous Area is a program and campfire hub focused on cultural, historical, and physical activities. 

## Rifle Range
Also located in the RATA area, the Rifle Range operates under strict safety protocols. Due to United States Forest Service fire restrictions on the mountain, the camp utilizes CO2 air rifles (pellets) instead of traditional .22 caliber firearms, which fully comply with the Rifle Shooting merit badge requirements.

## Santa Cruz Campsite
Nestled in the northwestern-most corner of the camp and surrounded by old-growth forest, the Santa Cruz campsite is great if you like a bit of rustic privacy or if your unit is too big for the Pima campsite. It comfortably holds up to 8 campers.

## Scoutcraft
Also referred to as Outdoor Skills, Scoutcraft is where traditional Scouting thrives. The area provides instruction in Wilderness Survival, Camping, Pioneering, Emergency Preparedness, and First Aid. It is also the starting point for the camp's day and night-eye orienteering compass courses.

## Smellables Shed
Bears can smell tempting scents for up to three miles and have the strength to break into most facilities in camp if they're motivated. All items that have an odor and might attract wildlife **must** be stored in the secured smellables shed, which is located next to the youth shower house. **Note:** The smellables shed is publicly accessible. Do not store any valuables in the Smellables Shed; if you need a secure place to store something, speak to camp administration.

## Staff Hill
During the summer camp season, Staff Hill is **Staff Only**, a designated space for the staff housing and time "off stage". Located on the north end of camp, it serves as the living quarters for the camp's employees. Since 2004, the area features high-quality cabins and four bathrooms with showers and flush toilets. More recently, the area was upgraded further to feature a full kitchen patio. By the way, during the rest of the year, you can rent out these facilities for your unit (or personal use) at https://catalinacouncil.org/outdoors/lawton/.

## STEM
While not traditional "camp" subjects, Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math are considered crucial skills for youth of this generation. The STEM program allows Scouts to learn everything from Game Design to Plumbing, greatly broadening the opportunities we can offer Scouts in camp. STEM is located in the Mike George Memorial Lodge (historically referred to as the Commissioner's Shack) between Navajo and the Camp Office.

## Yuma Campsite
Yuma is located near the southern center of the camp loop and accommodates up to 11 campers.

## Zuni Campsite
Zuni is a mid-sized campsite located centrally among the southern loop of sites. It can accommodate up to 12 campers.
