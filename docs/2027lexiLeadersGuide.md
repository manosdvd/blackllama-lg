**III.  Camp Policies**

**National Forest Land — USFS Special Use Permit**

Camp Lawton operates on National Forest land under a USFS Special Use Permit. Per NCAP AO-801, USFS permit conditions legally prevail over camp preferences. The following apply to all leaders, scouts, and visitors:

•   LEAVE NO TRACE: Pack out all trash. Stay on established trails. Do not trample native vegetation. Respect wildlife — observe from a distance, never feed.

•   CAMPFIRES: Subject to USFS fire restrictions at all times. Check with camp staff every morning. Fire bans can be issued without warning. Always have a no-flame alternative planned.

•   GROUND DISTURBANCE: No digging, trenching, or staking without Camp Ranger approval. Follow posted instructions for tent stake placement.

•   PETS: No pets within camp boundaries without USFS approval. Service animals with current documentation are permitted.

•   FIREARMS & FIREWORKS: Prohibited on National Forest land. No personal firearms may be brought to camp under any circumstances.

•   TRAIL USE: Group sizes on hiking trails may be restricted by your USFS permit. Confirm maximum group size for any off-road hike with the Camp Ranger before departure.

•   WASTE DISPOSAL: Dispose of all waste at designated central waste stations. Pack out trash. Never pour liquid waste on the ground.

•   WILDLIFE & FOOD: Store all food and scented items per USFS requirements. Report unusual wildlife activity to the Camp Ranger immediately.

**Youth Protection**

•   All adults in camp must be registered Scouting America members with current Youth Protection Training on file

•   Two-deep leadership required at all times — no one-on-one adult/youth contact, ever

•   Showers, latrines, and changing areas are separated by gender and age group

•   Report any suspected abuse immediately to the Camp Director and local law enforcement

•   Electronic device use by youth is restricted to designated areas and times as posted by camp

**Health & Safety**

•   Camp Health Officer (CHO) on duty all session hours per NCAP SQ-405. After-hours backup CHO designated — ask at check-in.

•   First aid kits located at: the Health Lodge, each program area, and carried by all trek leaders

•   Buddy System (NCAP HS-511) required for all scout movement throughout camp — brief your scouts at arrival

•   Emergency assembly point posted at your Ship and at each program area entrance

•   All serious incidents reported to Camp Director immediately per NCAP PD-112 tracking requirements

**Firearms & Range Activities**

Camp Lawton offers archery (PS-213) as a merit badge and program activity. Air rifle availability depends on two credentialed staff being present per PS-214 — confirm with Camp Director before your session.

•   No personal bows, arrows, or range equipment may be brought to camp without prior written approval from the Camp Director

•   The archery range location has been reviewed and approved consistent with the USFS Special Use Permit (AO-801)

•   Range SOPs are posted at the range entrance and available from the Range Director

**VI.  Leader Logistics**

**SPL & Leaders Meeting**

Held daily at 12:55 PM at the Captain’s Quarters (location posted in your leader packet). While leaders meet, scouts have supervised free time — trading post, campsite rest, or fellowship. Meetings run 30 minutes. All unit SPLs and adult leaders are expected to attend.

**Communication**

•   Emergency contact number for camp provided in your leader packet at check-in and posted in your Ship

•   Two-way radios issued to unit leaders daily — return each morning to the Trading Post

•   Cell service on National Forest land may be limited — camp communication system is the primary channel

•   All serious incidents must be reported to the Camp Director immediately per NCAP PD-112

**Thursday Evening — Campfire Kit Approval**

Thursday afternoon (7:25–8:25 PM) scouts organize their campfire kits for the Friday Camper Pirate Campfire. All skit content, songs, and stories must be reviewed and approved by a staff member before performance. This is a BSA requirement (PD-112 skit screening) and non-negotiable. Leaders should work with their scouts on content during Wednesday’s Pirate Shanty & Tale Prep block.

**Departure — Friday/Saturday**

•   Complete campsite inspection and check-out. Site must be left cleaner than found (USFS LNT requirement).

•   Return all issued equipment (radios, first aid kits, site keys) to the Trading Post.

•   Retrieve all medications from the Camp Health Officer with signature (HS-508).

•   Collect any retained valuables from the camp safe.

•   Receive departure clearance from Camp Director before leaving.

•   Exit via main entrance only. Speed limit in camp and on access roads: 10 MPH.

**Appendix:  Leave No Trace — National Forest Land Requirements**

All activities at Camp Lawton must follow Leave No Trace principles as required by the USFS Special Use Permit and NCAP PD-107. Please brief your scouts on these principles at arrival.

| LNT Principle | What It Means at Camp Lawton |
| :---- | :---- |
| **1\. Plan Ahead & Prepare** | Know your activity boundaries. Check fire restrictions every morning with staff. Brief scouts on USFS rules before going out. |
| **2\. Travel on Durable Surfaces** | Stay on established trails and cleared paths. Do not cut through native vegetation. Keep groups together. |
| **3\. Dispose of Waste Properly** | Pack out all trash — including craft materials, food wrappers, and broken equipment. Dispose of all waste at designated central waste stations. |
| **4\. Leave What You Find** | Do not collect rocks, plants, feathers, or natural materials. Do not move or stack rocks. Leave the forest as you found it. |
| **5\. Minimize Campfire Impacts** | Use established fire rings only. Check USFS fire restriction status daily. Fully extinguish all fires — cold to the touch. Have a no-flame backup planned for every evening campfire. |
| **6\. Respect Wildlife** | Observe animals from a distance. Never feed wildlife. Store all food and scented items properly. Report unusual wildlife to Camp Ranger. |
| **7\. Be Considerate of Others** | Respect quiet hours. Keep noise levels appropriate. Leave program areas cleaner than you found them. Share trails courteously. |
